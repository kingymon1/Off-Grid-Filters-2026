# Market & Audience Research — OffGrid Filters

> Market analysis, target audience, competitor landscape, and content opportunities.

---

## Target Audience Segments

### Primary: Health-Conscious Homeowners (35-55)
- **Demographics:** Middle to upper-middle income, homeowners, families with children
- **Buying triggers:** News about local water quality, PFAS contamination reports, new baby, moving to area with well water
- **Budget range:** $50–$500 for home filtration
- **Information needs:** What contaminants are in their water, which filter type removes them, installation complexity
- **Decision timeline:** 1-4 weeks of research before purchase

### Secondary: Budget-Conscious Renters (22-35)
- **Demographics:** Apartment dwellers, renters who can't modify plumbing
- **Buying triggers:** Bad-tasting tap water, wanting to stop buying bottled water, health consciousness
- **Budget range:** $20–$50 for pitcher; $200–$320 for countertop RO
- **Information needs:** No-install options, cost comparison vs bottled water, which pitcher is best
- **Decision timeline:** 1-7 days (impulse to short research)

### Tertiary: Outdoor Enthusiasts & Preppers (25-55)
- **Demographics:** Hikers, campers, off-grid enthusiasts, emergency preparedness community
- **Buying triggers:** Planning a trip, building a bug-out bag, emergency preparedness awareness
- **Budget range:** $10–$40 for portable filtration
- **Information needs:** Weight, reliability in the field, virus vs bacteria, water source safety
- **Decision timeline:** 1-2 weeks, often seasonal

### Quaternary: Appliance Maintainers (30-60)
- **Demographics:** Anyone needing to replace a refrigerator water filter
- **Buying triggers:** Fridge display shows "replace filter", water tastes off, scheduled maintenance
- **Budget range:** $17–$55 for replacement filter
- **Information needs:** Which filter fits their fridge, OEM vs aftermarket, certifications
- **Decision timeline:** Same day to 1 week (urgent maintenance need)

## Competitor Landscape

### Top Review Sites in Water Filter Niche

1. **WaterFilterGuru.com**
   - Comprehensive, data-heavy approach
   - Strong TDS testing and certification focus
   - Weakness: Overwhelming amount of content, hard to navigate
   - Gap: Limited budget-focused content

2. **Wirecutter (NYT) — Water Filters section**
   - Trusted brand, clean design, definitive recommendations
   - Weakness: Covers only a few products per category; slow to update
   - Gap: No portable/survival filter coverage

3. **BOS (Best Osmosis Systems)**
   - RO-focused niche site
   - Weakness: Only covers RO systems, not broader filtration
   - Gap: No pitcher/portable/fridge filter content

4. **FilterReviews.com**
   - Broad but shallow coverage
   - Weakness: Dated design, less authoritative content
   - Gap: Poor comparison content

5. **TheBerkey.com / BerkeyFilters.com**
   - Brand-specific — only covers Berkey products
   - Gap: Not an independent review site

### Our Competitive Advantages
1. **Breadth:** Cover ALL six categories (countertop, under-sink, fridge, replacement, survival, whole-house)
2. **Honesty:** Every product has real pros AND cons — not an ad disguised as a review
3. **Data focus:** Cost-per-gallon analysis, certification comparisons, spec tables
4. **Cross-category guidance:** Help users decide between a pitcher and an RO system, not just within categories
5. **Emergency preparedness angle:** Under-served niche with high search volume
6. **Modern design:** Most competitors have dated, cluttered sites

### High-Volume Keywords

- "best under sink water filter" (12K monthly)

- "brita vs pur" (6K monthly)

- "pfas water filter" (8K monthly, growing)

### Question-Based Searches (FAQ/Guide Opportunities)
- "is tap water safe to drink" (12K monthly)
- "do water filters remove pfas" (3K monthly)
- "what does a water filter remove" (2.5K monthly)
- "how often to replace water filter" (4K monthly)
- "brita filter vs zero water" (3K monthly)
- "best water filter for lead" (2K monthly)
- "water filter for baby formula" (1.5K monthly)

### Seasonal Patterns
- **January/February:** New Year health resolutions → pitcher/RO purchases spike
- **Spring:** Water quality reports published → under-sink and whole-house interest
- **Summer:** Camping/hiking season → portable filter demand peaks
- **September:** Back-to-school, emergency preparedness month → survival filters
- **November/December:** Holiday gifting → premium products (Waterdrop, Bluevua)

### Under-Served Content Gaps We Fill
1. **Cost-per-gallon analysis** — no competitor does this systematically
2. **Cross-category comparisons** — "pitcher vs countertop RO" guide
3. **Emergency preparedness water filtration** — growing demand, few quality resources
4. **Refrigerator filter compatibility guides** — confusing space, people need help
5. **Baby/infant water safety** — safety-critical, high-trust topic
6. **Off-grid/RV water filtration** — niche but passionate, underserved audience
